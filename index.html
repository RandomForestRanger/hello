Hi There, I'm Martin Bekker

I’m a father, husband, AI-enthusiast, computational social scientist and community development planner. That and a long-suffering Lions fan.

The transformative possibilities of AI excite me most, and I’m most concerned about growing wealth inequality and shrinking public participation in political decision-making. I’m all about using data for advocacy, whether for academic audiences, industry specialists, online forums or Op-Ed platforms. I hold an MSc in Development Management and an MA in Conflict Resolution. In my PhD I ask “why do people protest and riot?” and use machine learning to access the world’s largest available dataset of protest events to model and test different sociological, political, and demographic theories. I help teach a bit at the University of Johannesburg. I’ve been to some conferences.

I love traveling. Johannesburg, South Africa, is my home.

Previously I was a strategist leading a small research team for a traditional African kingdom, but the call of the wild open fonrtiers in AI ethics, where I can make a meaningful impact, has won me over.

I'm an open source developer and designer living in Oakland, California. Far, far away from my East Coast US roots. I work at GitHub, mentor at programming events (NodeSchool.io, Patchwork) and do some conferences, too.

Previously I was an urban designer, but the call of the wild open tech world would win me over.
